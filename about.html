About Green Agro Health Initiative

A youth-led Community Based Organisation transforming agricultural waste into sustainable solutions for soil fertility, clean energy, and community empowerment in Kenya.

Our Vision & Mission

Organization Details

Name & Registration

Green Agro Health Initiative (GAHI) is registered as a community-based organization (CBO) under the Community Groups (Registration) Regulations, 2025 and complies with all relevant Kenyan laws.

Location

Our headquarters is located in Nairobi County, Kenya, with operations extending to farming communities across the country. GAHI may establish regional offices, project sites, or coordination centers as approved by the Board.

Our Principles

GAHI operates as a non-profit, non-political, non-religious, and non-ethnic organization, dedicated to the public good and community development.

Our Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To build resilient, healthy, and sustainable farming communities through climate-smart agriculture, responsible waste management, and innovative community-driven solutions where agriculture meets health and sustainability.

Our Mission

To promote sustainable waste management, turning waste into opportunity, regenerative agriculture, public health awareness, and climate resilience through innovation, capacity building, and inclusive community-led programs.

Our Guiding Principles

Integrity & Transparency

Operate with honesty, openness, and accountability in governance and program implementation.

Community Participation

Ensure broad community involvement and ownership in decision-making and project execution.

Environmental Stewardship

Promote conservation and innovative waste-to-value approaches to protect natural resources.

Youth & Women Inclusion

Actively include and empower youth and women in leadership and all programs.

Innovation & Knowledge

Base interventions on scientific research and innovative solutions for sustainable development.

Equity & Non-Discrimination

Ensure fairness, equality, and non-discrimination in all activities.

Our Founding Story

Born from Passion for Sustainable Agriculture

GAHI was founded by a group of passionate young Kenyans who recognized the interconnected challenges of agricultural waste management, antimicrobial resistance, and climate change in their communities.

Seeing the potential in transforming poultry waste from a pollutant into a resource for soil fertility and clean energy, the founders came together in 2025 to establish GAHI as a platform for youth-led innovation in sustainable agriculture.

Our approach combines traditional farming knowledge with modern scientific methods to create practical solutions that benefit both the environment and local communities. We believe that youth and women are key drivers of sustainable change, and we're committed to providing them with the tools and knowledge to lead climate action in their communities.
