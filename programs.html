Our 14 Core Objectives

Click on each objective to learn more about our specific goals and future projects

1 Poultry Waste Management

Promote safe and sustainable collection, treatment, and utilization of poultry and organic waste (e.g. raw chicken manure) by supporting farmers with free manure distribution, encouraging proper composting and developing future waste to value innovations that enhance soil fertility, reduce pollution and support regenerative agriculture.

Future Project Examples:

  • Establish community composting centers in 5 counties
  • Develop mobile app for waste collection scheduling
  • Create training manuals for proper manure management
  • Pilot waste-to-fertilizer conversion units
2 Climate-Smart Agriculture

Support and train farmers in climate smart and regenerative farming practices, such as conservation agriculture, agroforestry, and efficient irrigation, to enhance food security, environmental conservation, boost productivity and strengthen climate adaptation.

Future Project Examples:

  • Establish demonstration farms for climate-smart techniques
  • Develop training programs on water conservation methods
  • Create agroforestry nurseries with drought-resistant species
  • Organize farmer field schools on conservation agriculture
3 Holistic One Health Approach & AMR Awareness

Advance community health education by raising awareness on antimicrobial resistance (AMR), animal source food safety, promoting responsible use of antibiotics in animals and humans to protect public health.

Future Project Examples:

  • Develop AMR awareness campaign materials in local languages
  • Organize community workshops on responsible antibiotic use
  • Create educational videos on food safety practices
  • Establish partnerships with local health centers for joint awareness programs
4 Waste-to-Energy & Briquette Production

Develop sustainable waste to energy initiatives, including the production of clean briquettes from poultry manure mixed with rice husks and other residues, to reduce environmental pollution, promote circular economy solutions and provide affordable alternative fuel sources for communities.

Future Project Examples:

  • Establish small-scale briquette production units
  • Develop training programs on briquette making
  • Create partnerships with institutions for fuel supply
  • Research optimal briquette mixtures for maximum efficiency
5 Youth and Women & Community Empowerment

Empower youth, women, and smallholder farmers through education, vocational training, leadership development, and income-generating activities, enabling them to lead climate action and address challenges like poverty and illiteracy.

Future Project Examples:

  • Establish youth entrepreneurship programs in green economy
  • Create women-led cooperatives for waste management enterprises
  • Develop vocational training in sustainable agriculture techniques
  • Organize leadership workshops for young community leaders
6 Environmental Protection

Promote activities that protect the environment, enhance biodiversity and reduce pollution through responsible waste management, soil conservation and restoration initiatives.

Future Project Examples:

  • Establish tree planting campaigns in degraded areas
  • Develop community-based soil conservation programs
  • Create awareness campaigns on plastic pollution reduction
  • Organize clean-up drives in farming communities
7 Partnership & Collaboration

Build strong partnerships with government agencies, NGOs, research institutions, academic institutions, and international organizations to share knowledge, leverage resources, and scale sustainable agricultural, climate and health solutions.

Future Project Examples:

  • Establish MoUs with agricultural research institutions
  • Create partnerships with county governments for waste management
  • Develop collaboration networks with other youth-led CBOs
  • Organize joint workshops with health organizations on AMR
8 Advocacy & Research

Undertake advocacy, research, and awareness campaigns on climate change adaptation, soil and environmental health, AMR, sustainable food systems, and related development issues.

9 Resource Mobilization

Mobilize financial and non-financial resources, including grants, donations, sponsorships, and partnerships, to support GAHI's programs and community projects.

10 Community Education

Facilitate lectures, workshops, and community events on public health topics and environmental stewardship to improve community well-being.

11 Collaborative Engagement

Stimulate and involve other organizations (NGOs, CBOs, self-help groups) in joint initiatives that improve livelihoods and protect the environment.

12 Local Employment & Skills Development

Utilize local skills and labor for construction, maintenance, and repair of GAHI projects and assets to create employment and build community capacity.

13 Support Services & Capacity Building

Support the training of local farmers, youth groups and community organizations with technical assistance, mentorship and practical skills to improve productivity, livelihoods and resilience.

14 General Activities

Perform all other lawful activities necessary, possible, or conducive to the attainment of GAHI's mission, vision and ongoing objectives.
